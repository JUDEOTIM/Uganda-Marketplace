1. Start Small

Begin with 20–50 chickens to reduce risk and gain experience.

    Why start small?

  • Easier management
  • Lower startup cost
  • Opportunity to learn

💡 Example:

Start with 30 birds, learn feeding, disease control, and marketing → then expand to 100+.

2. Choose the Right Breed

Your goal determines the type of chicken:

  • Layers: For egg production and they start laying at ~5 months
  • Broilers: For meat production (grow fast, ready in 6–8 weeks)
  • Kuroiler/Local breeds: Best for beginners

Local breeds are hardy, disease-resistant, and require less intensive feeding.

3. Poultry Housing

Proper housing ensures healthy growth and protection.

  • Good ventilation (fresh air prevents disease)
  • Dry flooring (use sawdust)
  • Protection from predators (dogs, snakes, thieves)
  • Adequate space to avoid overcrowding(0.1–0.2 m² per bird)

💡 Tip:

Use the deep litter system (cheap and effective for beginners).

4. Clean Water Supply

Water is critical for chicken survival.

  • Provide fresh water daily
  • Clean drinkers regularly
  • Avoid contamination

5. Feeding

Feed accounts for 60–70% of costs.

  • Starter feed (0–4 weeks)
  • Grower feed (4–8 weeks)
  • Layer mash (egg production)

Feed chickens 2–3 times daily and minimize waste.

6. Health & Vaccination

Prevent diseases through proper care.

  • Vaccinate regularly
  • Maintain cleanliness
  • Limit visitors (biosecurity)

Common diseases include Newcastle and Gumboro.

7. Market Planning

Plan how you will sell before starting.

  • Local markets
  • Restaurants
  • Neighbors

Eggs provide daily income while broilers provide bulk income.
